Contextualized Text Representation for Diacritic-Free Hebrew TTS

Anonymous Submission to Interspeech 2024

Abstract

We tackle the task of text-to-speech (TTS) in Hebrew. Traditional Hebrew contains Diacritics (“Nikud”), which dictates the way individuals should pronounce given words, however modern Hebrew rarely uses them. The lack of Diacritics in modern Hebrew results in readers expected to conclude the correct pronunciation and understand which phonemes to use based on the context. This imposes an inheriting challenge on TTS systems to accurately map between text to audio. In this study, we propose a Diacritics-Free TTS method, which leverages contextualized text representation together with a word-piece tokenizer. We demonstrate the efficiency of the proposed method considering a TTS method operating over discrete speech representation optimized using in-the-wild weakly supervised recordings. Results suggest the proposed method is superior to the evaluated baselines considering both content preservation, and naturality of the generated speech.
