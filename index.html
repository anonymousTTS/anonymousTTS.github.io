A Language Modeling Approach to Diacritic-Free Hebrew TTS

Anonymous Submission to Interspeech 2024

Abstract

We tackle the task of text-to-speech (TTS) in Hebrew. Traditional Hebrew contains Diacritics ('Nikud'), which dictates the way individuals should pronounce given words, however modern Hebrew rarely uses them. The lack of Diacritics in modern Hebrew results in readers expected to conclude the correct pronunciation and understand which phonemes to use based on the context. This imposes an inheriting challenge on TTS systems to accurately map between text-to-speech. In this study, we propose to adopt a language modeling Diacritics-Free TTS approach, for the task of Hebrew TTS. The langauge model (LM) operates on discrete speech representation and condition on a WordPiece tokenizer. We optimize the proposed method using in-the-wild weakly supervised recordings, and compare it to several Diacritic based Hebrew TTS systems. Results suggest the proposed method is superior to the evaluated baselines considering both content preservation, and neutrality of the generated speech.
